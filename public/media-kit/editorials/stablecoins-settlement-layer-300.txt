# Why Stablecoins Are the Settlement Layer for AI Agents

The payment infrastructure that runs the global economy was built for a simple assumption: a human being initiates every transaction. Card networks, bank transfers, manual authorisation cycles — all designed for people.

AI agents are not people. They don't fill in forms, they don't wait 72 hours for settlement, and they can't absorb a 6% fee on a four-dollar cross-border purchase.

That's the problem. Here's the solution.

## The Fit

Stablecoins — digital currencies pegged 1:1 to the US dollar — settle in seconds, cost less than 0.1% per transaction, work identically across borders, and run on smart-contract platforms where payment logic can be programmed directly into the transaction. No intermediaries. No clearing houses. No waiting.

The stablecoin market now exceeds $200 billion in active settlement liquidity. Not speculative value — deployed capital sitting on blockchain networks, ready for programmatic access.

For an AI agent that needs to chain dozens of transactions per hour across multiple countries, this isn't a better option. It's the only viable option.

## The Signal You Can't Ignore

Mastercard has onboarded 85 crypto partners. Stripe — the company that walked away from crypto in 2018 — has come back and is building native stablecoin support into its APIs. Visa is piloting stablecoin settlement across 80 million merchants.

Three companies. Trillions in annual volume. Same conclusion, reached independently: the settlement layer for autonomous commerce will be stablecoin-based.

The infrastructure is live. The economics are decisive. The only variable left is how quickly individual businesses connect to it.
