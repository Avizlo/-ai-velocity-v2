# The Settlement Layer Is Live: Stripe, Visa, and Mastercard Converge on Agentic Payments

Something unusual is happening in payments. Three of the largest financial networks on earth — Stripe, Visa, and Mastercard — have each independently concluded that AI agents will become a primary initiator of commercial transactions. Not eventually. Now.

This wasn't coordinated. No consortium. No joint whitepaper. Three companies processing trillions annually looked at the same data and arrived at the same answer.

## What They're Building

Mastercard has onboarded 85 crypto partners and is building its Multi-Token Network for programmable, stablecoin-based settlement. Stripe — after walking away from crypto for six years — is back, embedding native stablecoin support into the same APIs millions of businesses already use. Visa is solving the merchant side: its Universal Payment Channel lets merchants accept agent-initiated stablecoin payments without changing a thing about their existing setup.

Each company is attacking the problem from a different angle. All three are building toward the same destination.

## Why It Matters

Traditional cross-border payments cost 6% to 8% per transaction. Stablecoin settlement costs less than 0.1%. For AI agents executing hundreds of transactions daily across borders, that's the difference between a viable business model and a broken one.

The stablecoin market exceeds $200 billion in deployed liquidity. The regulatory frameworks — MiCA in Europe, advancing legislation in the US, FCA guidance in the UK — are in place.

When the incumbents agree on the direction, the question stops being whether this happens and starts being how quickly you connect to it.
